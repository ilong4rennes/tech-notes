# Week 6 — Newton II and Newtonian Anomalies

## Newton's Achievement

Newton unified terrestrial and celestial physics.

The same laws explain:

* falling apples
* planetary orbits

### Laws of Motion

1. Inertia
2. Force equals mass times acceleration
3. Action and reaction

### Universal Gravitation

Every object attracts every other object with a force proportional to:

* their masses
* inverse square of their distance

### Philosophical Significance

Newton demonstrated that a **small set of universal laws** could explain a vast range of phenomena.

This created a powerful vision of the universe as a **mathematical system governed by universal laws**.

Newton's theory also raised philosophical questions.

Gravity appeared to act **at a distance**, with no visible mechanism.

Critics argued this resembled mystical or occult forces.

Newton's response was methodological.

Science should describe **how nature behaves**, even if the underlying mechanism remains unknown.

This marks an important methodological stance:

**Predictive power can justify scientific theories even when deeper explanations are incomplete.**

## Newtonian Anomalies

Even Newton's framework encountered difficulties.

Examples include:

* irregularities in planetary motion
* unexplained perturbations

### Philosophical Significance

No scientific theory perfectly explains everything.

Even highly successful theories contain anomalies.

This reinforces Kuhn's insight:

Scientific paradigms remain stable despite anomalies until a better alternative emerges.
