# Part 1: Measuring neurons and the brain

## Why Study Perception?
- **Basic science**: explains how mental representations arise from the physical world.
- **Quantitative data**: measures human abilities (normal range, age differences, impairments) with applications.
- **Neuroscience**: shows how perception links neural processes with mental functions.
- **Broader understanding**: perception underlies almost all behavior (action, emotion, thought).

## Neurons and Neural Firing
- **Neuron structure**: dendrites, soma, axon, terminals; many structural variations exist.
- **Neural firing (spikes)**:
    - Triggered by chemical stimulation at receptors.
    - Positive ions enter, neuron reaches threshold, fires an action potential.
    - Brief electrical signal travels down axon; ions pumped out to reset.
- **Information flow**: spikes cross synapses via neurotransmitters, creating neural networks.

## Encoding Information

Two main ways neurons convey perceptual information:

1. **Total spike activity**
    - Count of spikes over time.
    - Stronger stimulus → more spikes, faster accumulation.

2. **Precise spike timing**
    - Timing patterns of firing relative to input.
    - Example: auditory neurons fire in sync with sound wave frequency (“neural entrainment”).
    - Allows encoding of both fast and slow rhythms.

## Key Insight
- Perception is grounded in how neurons fire and communicate.
- Both **intensity (spike count)** and **timing (entrainment)** carry critical information for representing sensory inputs.
